Computer Science as a Discipline

Definition and Overview

Computer Science is the study of computing and algorithmic processes, encompassing both their theoretical foundations and practical applications in hardware and software. It explores concepts such as design, analysis, and efficiency while addressing the key question of what can and cannot be automated.

Computing Profession

The computing profession consists of the professionals and institutions that communicate globally to process information, solve problems, address human issues, and program modern machines. It encompasses a wide range of disciplines, including software development, data science, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and more.

History of Computer Science

In the 1940s, computer science as a discipline was born, with the first computers being invented in the 1930s. By the 1960s, the field of computing became knowledgeable enough for universities to create departments and programs related to computer science.

Pioneers of Computer Science
Alan Turing

Father of Computer Science; shortened World War 2 by two years by decoding German secret messages through the Enigma machine

Kurt Godel

Logician and mathematician who set the rules for set theory and logic concepts relevant to computing

Ada Lovelace

The world's first computer programmer; developed the first algorithm for a computer to perform a task

Vannevar Bush

Engineer who developed analog computers meant to perform mathematical calculations such as derivations

Howard Aiken

Invented the Harvard Mark I, the forerunner of the modern electronic digital computer

Konrad Zuse

Invented the Z3, the world's first fully functional and programmable digital computer

John von Neumann

Developed the notion of "von Neumann architecture" and contributed to the fields of linear programming

Computing Disciplines and Majors

Definition

Computing involves creating and using computers to perform various tasks, including designing hardware and software, processing information, and conducting research. With its broad scope spanning the fields of mathematics, engineering, and business, computing professionals need to have a specialization - be it Computer Engineering, Computer Science, Information Systems, Information Technology, or Software Engineering.

5 Computing Disciplines and Majors
Computer Engineering

Computer Engineering combines electrical engineering and computer science to design, build, and maintain hardware and software systems. It focuses on hardware and its interaction with software to create and improve computer-based devices.

Computer Science

Computer Science, rooted in mathematics and logic, emphasizes theory more than other computing fields. It covers topics like algorithms, programming, data structures, software design, operating systems, machine learning, and cybersecurity. With a strong focus on algorithms and programming, it bridges theoretical concepts with practical software development.

Information Systems

Information Systems focuses on the practical use of computing in business and commerce, delivering information and communication services to support decision-making. It integrates technology with business processes to help organizations achieve their goals efficiently.

Information Technology

Information Technology focuses on managing and implementing computer systems and software to process data. It emphasizes practical tasks like configuring hardware, troubleshooting, and providing technical support while addressing user needs through technology. Key areas include human-computer interaction, databases, and networks.

Software Engineering

Software Engineering focuses on designing, developing, testing, and maintaining reliable software systems to meet specific user needs. It emphasizes the software life cycle and has practical applications in fields like defense, aerospace, entertainment, avionics, operating systems, and telecommunications.
